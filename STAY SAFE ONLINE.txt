Stay Safe Online: Essential Checklist for Students
UPI Safety (The "PIN" Rule): Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is strictly for sending or deducting money from your account. If someone asks for your PIN to "verify" a payment to you, it is a scam.

2. QR Code Caution: Only scan QR codes at trusted, physical merchant locations. Avoid scanning any QR code sent via WhatsApp or email to "claim a prize" or "refund," as this can authorize an immediate transfer out of your account.

3. Use Official Domains Only: When applying for scholarships, internships, or exams, ensure the website ends in .gov.in or .ac.in. Scammers often create "mirror" sites that look identical to official portals to steal your data.

4. Protect Your Primary ID: Avoid sharing full photos of your Aadhaar or PAN card on social media or unsecured apps. Use a Masked Aadhaar (where only the last 4 digits are visible) whenever possible for general verification.

5. Secure Your Project Repositories: If you use platforms like GitHub or Kaggle, ensure you never hardcode passwords, API keys, or personal credentials into your public code. These can be scraped by bots within seconds.

6. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Turn on 2FA for your university email, LinkedIn, and primary Gmail accounts. This adds a vital second layer of security that a password alone cannot provide.

7. Verify Social Media "Job Offers": Be skeptical of high-paying "work from home" offers sent via DM or WhatsApp. Always verify the recruiter's identity on LinkedIn or the company's official career page before sharing any documents.

8. Report Cyber Crimes Immediately: If you are a victim of financial fraud or identity theft, report it immediately to the National Cyber Crime Portal at cybercrime.gov.in.

9. Use the 24/7 Helpline: For urgent assistance with financial cyber fraud, call the national helpline number 1930 instantly. Reporting within the first hour (the "Golden Hour") increases the chance of recovering lost funds.
